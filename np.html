The Boy Who Lived

by J.K. Rowling

Harry Potter, an orphaned boy, discovers on his 11th birthday that he is a wizard. Raised by his abusive Muggle relatives, the Dursleys, Harry learns that his magical heritage comes with a destiny intertwined with the dark wizard Voldemort.

Sorting Hat Decisions

A House for Every Student

At Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, students are sorted into one of four houses: Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, or Slytherin. Harry is placed in Gryffindor, where he finds friendship and courage.

The Philosopher's Stone

Immunity and Immortality

The Philosopher’s Stone, a magical object granting immortality and wealth, becomes the center of a perilous quest as Voldemort seeks to reclaim power. Harry and his friends work to protect it from dark forces.

Friendship and Bravery

Ron and Hermione

Harry's journey is strengthened by his loyal friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. Together, they face dangers and unlock the secrets of their magical school.
